A bit about myself.

Hey there, my name is Eddy Peng, and I am currently a sophomore at the University of Washington. I am majoring in Informatics (Human-computer interaction) and minoring in Art history.

I have always been interested in business, research, psychology, and design. So, when I discovered the UX field during my senior year of high school, I knew that it was the right career for me.

Art History has taught me the importance of function in contrast to form. Therefore, I am able to design simple yet efficient products that makes people happy.

On my free time, I like to photograph clouds and architecture.

My Values.

Respectfulness towards peers and their ideas.

Everyone has their own personal perspectives, experiences, and ideas. However, we are unable to read each others minds to determine what they are thinking. Keeping this in mind, I give other peoples' ideas the time to shine by listening and admiring them for their experiences.

Its okay to make mistakes.

We humans often make mistakes as it is part of our nature. However, it is okay. By making mistakes, it allows us to learn from our mistakes and make it a part of our human experiences and reflect on how we could prevent it from reoccurring later on.

Self-respect.

As a college student, I understand there are areas of expertise where I have little to no knowledge. That will be true for me even after I graduate. With UX design rapidly evolving as a field, there will always be room for growth. By keeping an open mind, I am able to learn from fellow peers and mentors without the fear of feeling insecure.
