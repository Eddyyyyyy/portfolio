About

During the summer, I interned at Ziggurat to work on their first product: Zivolve. Zivolve is a mobile investment application that uses AI to help investors make smart and informed decisions.

www.zivolve.com

Tools

Figma

Duration

May - Jul 2022

Why Zivolve?

Hedge funds get rich while 95% of market traders lose $$.

In contrast to large hedge funds, the average market trader does not have the experience or the technology needed to make decisions. Because of this, the playing field is heavily favored towards large hedge funds and corporations. Zivolve’s goal is to level the playing field by using AI to suggest decisions based on market behavior and trends.

Tasks

Market research -> UI design -> User Flow -> Iterations.

As an interaction design intern, I was to analyze well-established stock applications such as Robinhood, Webull, and Ameritrade. Next, I took the relevant functionality and put my own twist on it in a way that enhanced the UX and made the UI consistent with the current Zivolve app.

Then, I created user flows to communicate my ideas to the CEO and developers. Finally, after daily standups and weekly meetings, iterations to the flow and UI were made after some considerations.

Impact

I laid the groundwork for most of the features to come.

During my time at Ziggurat, my co-intern and I designed many features. Although some of our new designs have been developed, most of the features are still in the agile development process and will be rolled out in the next 5 versions of the product. Many of the features I designed are essential to the product’s success.

Learnings

I learned to design and simplify the complexities of trading.

Coming into the internship, I knew next to nothing about asset trading. I had to learn how everything worked through talking to the CEO and through my own research. It was important that I understood trading because the product’s functionality needed to be intuitive. The displayed data (such as the various charts, graphs, and tables) needed to make sense to the average joe.
