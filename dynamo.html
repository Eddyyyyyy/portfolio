Project Objective.

The transition from high school to college is not easy. Balancing a heavier workload while keeping up with exercise, friends, and work make it extremely overwhelming for many students. With all this stress, it is not surprising that students face mental health issues.

This problem inspired me and my team to create a solution to lift some of the responsibilities off students' shoulders, so they can focus on enjoying the college experience.

“How might we reduce the stresses of college life on freshmen?”

My Roles.

Although I had a hand in every aspect of the project, I mainly focused on revising the wireframes to be more consistent, creating the whole prototype along with its animations, and product branding.

The Process.

Competitors had Simple UI, the ability to Send Reminders, and a arduous task input process.

To jump start our ideas, we looked at what other people have done to address student stress. We decided conduct research on scheduling, trackers, and to-do list products because organization leads to a simpler and stress-free lifestyle. We found three products, myHomework, Habit Tracker, and remember the milk. All products come with calendars, lists, progress monitoring, reminders, and an easy to understand user interface. It is important to note that all products are extremely manual, meaning the user has to input everything. Thus, these products can be time-consuming to use on a daily basis and even stressful due to the pressure that students are already in.

40 College Freshmen Surveyed and 5 Interviewees.

Survey results of University of Washington freshmen.

We conducted a survey on Freshmen at our university (since it was convienient) and we were interested in the workload increase from the high school level to the university level. Participants were also asked about whether they felt stress during their transition to college and how they handled that stress. These questions matter to us because we needed confirmation that college life was much tougher than high school life. Now that we have the statistics to back up our project objective, it was time to dive deeper to understand and empathize with individual students.
